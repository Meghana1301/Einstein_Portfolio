About

Albert Einstein(1879–1955) was a theoretical physicist best known for developing the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics (alongside quantum mechanics). His famous equation, E = mc², expresses the relationship between energy and mass. Einstein's work revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and energy, and he received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which was crucial in the development of quantum theory. In addition to his scientific achievements, Einstein was a humanitarian and advocate for civil rights, education, and international peace. His brilliant mind and iconic image, often seen with unruly hair and playful expressions, made him one of the most recognized and celebrated figures in history.
